<p><em>"We see 10,000&nbsp;critical vulnerabilities!"&nbsp;</em></p>

<p><em>"We patched everything last week!"&nbsp;</em></p>

<p>This conversation happens in enterprise IT departments every single day. Security teams present dashboards filled with red alerts. IT teams show deployment reports at 98% success. Both teams are looking at real data.&nbsp;Both are absolutely correct.&nbsp;And both are&nbsp;totally&nbsp;blind to what's actually happening across the endpoint environment.&nbsp;</p>

<p>This&nbsp;isn't&nbsp;a&nbsp;people&nbsp;problem — your teams&nbsp;aren't&nbsp;incompetent.&nbsp;It's&nbsp;not a process problem — your workflows&nbsp;aren't&nbsp;broken.&nbsp;It's&nbsp;a technology problem:&nbsp;you're&nbsp;asking two teams to manage the same risk using systems that&nbsp;show them different realities.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Security teams are given one version of reality through vulnerability scanners and threat intelligence. Meanwhile, IT teams see things differently when looking at their device management and patch deployment reports.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>The tricky part is that both views can be&nbsp;correct&nbsp;in isolation and&nbsp;still&nbsp;be&nbsp;misleading&nbsp;in practice.&nbsp;That's&nbsp;how you end up in the familiar stalemate: security reports thousands of critical vulnerabilities; IT reports that patches are successfully deployed. The disconnect lives in the gap between those systems.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>Why&nbsp;IT&nbsp;and&nbsp;security&nbsp;are misaligned on patching&nbsp;&nbsp;</h2>

<p>Most organizations approach&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/endpoint-management-ownership-it-security-governance">patching misalignment between IT and security</a>&nbsp;by improving communication between IT and security. They schedule more meetings. They create escalation paths. They implement SLAs. And six months later,&nbsp;they're&nbsp;having the exact same argument with better PowerPoint slides.</p>

<p>Here's&nbsp;what nobody wants to admit:&nbsp;you&nbsp;can't&nbsp;collaborate your way out of a data fragmentation problem. When IT and security are working from fundamentally different inventories of what exists,&nbsp;what's&nbsp;vulnerable&nbsp;and&nbsp;what's&nbsp;been fixed, adding more coordination overhead just slows down an already broken process.&nbsp;</p>

<p>This is why the same conversation plays out again and again inside many organizations.&nbsp;Both teams are confident in their data, and both are “right” within the narrow context of the tools they rely on.&nbsp;</p>

<p>And&nbsp;that’s&nbsp;the problem. While both views are “right,” neither reflects the full lifecycle of risk. Vulnerability data&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;always reflect whether affected devices are managed or reachable. Patch reports&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;always account for unmanaged,&nbsp;misclassified&nbsp;or&nbsp;newly discovered endpoints that still have access to corporate resources.&nbsp;What’s missing is a reliable answer to the only question that actually matters: which endpoints are exposed right now?&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Technology silos create conflicting realities&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Most enterprises manage endpoints through&nbsp;a hodgepodge of&nbsp;systems that&nbsp;have evolved&nbsp;independently over time, each capturing only a fragment of reality.&nbsp;</p>

<p>One system may surface critical exposure without knowing whether the device is&nbsp;being managed. Another may confirm successful remediation without accounting for newly discovered or misclassified endpoints that still have access.&nbsp;The result? No reliable way to trace risk from detection through deployment to actual exposure.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Consider this:&nbsp;the&nbsp;average organization manages only 60% of their edge devices, according to Ivanti's&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/borderless-security" target="_blank">Securing the Borderless Digital Landscape Report</a>. That means 40% of potential entry points exist outside IT's view and outside their patch workflows. Security sees them.&nbsp;IT&nbsp;doesn't. That's&nbsp;your&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/attack-surface-visibility-gaps">vulnerability gap</a>.&nbsp;Without that continuity, teams are forced to reconcile partial views manually. Data gets debated instead of&nbsp;acted&nbsp;on.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Different data views lead to friction&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Imagine&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;Monday morning: Security discovers a critical zero-day in a widely used VPN client. They send an urgent alert to IT: "30,000 vulnerable endpoints detected — patch immediately."&nbsp;</p>

<p>IT checks their deployment console: <em>"VPN client already updated across 28,000 devices last Thursday."</em></p>

<p>Both statements are true. Security is scanning the entire network — including contractor laptops, BYOD devices&nbsp;and&nbsp;systems that&nbsp;briefly&nbsp;connected to the VPN but&nbsp;aren't&nbsp;under IT management. IT patched everything in their device inventory.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Meanwhile, 2,000 genuinely vulnerable endpoints&nbsp;remain&nbsp;exposed because they exist in Security's view but not IT's.&nbsp;The patch that should have taken 24 hours now requires three days of manual reconciliation.&nbsp;</p>

<p>When IT and security&nbsp;operate&nbsp;from different data sources, misaligned&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/vulnerability-prioritization-guide">vulnerability management priorities</a>&nbsp;are inevitable.&nbsp;Security teams focus on vulnerability counts, severity&nbsp;scores&nbsp;and&nbsp;exploit intelligence. IT teams prioritize deployment success, system&nbsp;stability&nbsp;and&nbsp;user impact. Both perspectives are necessary, but without a shared frame of reference, they pull in different directions.&nbsp;</p>

<p>What follows&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;just tension;&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;decision paralysis. Remediation slows while teams reconcile inventories,&nbsp;validate&nbsp;findings&nbsp;and&nbsp;argue about scope. Vulnerabilities&nbsp;remain&nbsp;open longer than they should, not because patches&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;available, but because&nbsp;there’s&nbsp;no single view that connects detection,&nbsp;deployment&nbsp;and&nbsp;exposure.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>The&nbsp;risk of misaligned patching priorities&nbsp;</h2>

<p>Misalignment slows collaboration, but more so, it creates measurable risk that extends well beyond internal friction.</p>

<p>Ivanti’s&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/aem" target="_blank">Autonomous Endpoint Management research</a>&nbsp;reflects this challenge in practice:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>38% of IT professionals report difficulty tracking patch status.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>35% struggle to meet remediation timelines due to incomplete endpoint visibility.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>When vulnerabilities&nbsp;remain&nbsp;open longer than necessary, the window of exposure grows. Attackers&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;wait.&nbsp;The&nbsp;<a href="https://www.cisa.gov/known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog" rel="noopener" target="_blank">CISA KEV catalog</a>&nbsp;reveals the&nbsp;difficult truth: 30% of vulnerabilities being actively exploited right now were originally&nbsp;disclosed&nbsp;more than five years ago.&nbsp;</p>

<p>That's&nbsp;not a patching problem;&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;a&nbsp;visibility problem. Organizations&nbsp;aren't&nbsp;ignoring available patches;&nbsp;they're&nbsp;missing the endpoints that still need them.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Prolonged&nbsp;exposure&nbsp;windows and&nbsp;breach&nbsp;risk&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Fragmentation stretches&nbsp;exposure&nbsp;windows in subtle ways. Devices that were never enrolled in management platforms, such as shadow BYOD, unsecured contractor&nbsp;devices&nbsp;or&nbsp;remote endpoints outside the traditional perimeter, often go unnoticed.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p><a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/borderless-security" target="_blank">Research from Ivanti</a>&nbsp;shows&nbsp;that only one in three employers&nbsp;have&nbsp;implemented zero trust network access for remote workers, leaving significant gaps in visibility across distributed environments.&nbsp;Newly discovered endpoints appear after patch reports are generated. Systems drift out of compliance between scan cycles. Each delay compounds the risk, extending the time attackers&nbsp;have to&nbsp;weaponize known weaknesses.</p>

<h2>Common&nbsp;post-patch&nbsp;issues and IT&nbsp;ticket&nbsp;overload&nbsp;</h2>

<p>Even when patches are deployed on schedule, manual patching often creates downstream issues. Failed updates, broken agents, performance&nbsp;problems&nbsp;and&nbsp;unexpected reboots trigger support tickets and emergency fixes. What starts as a security task quickly becomes an operational drain.&nbsp;</p>

<p>IT teams spend time resolving predictable failures instead of&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/endpoint-management-ownership-it-security-governance">improving endpoint posture</a>. Security teams see delays as unresolved risk. Users associate patching with disruption. That friction persists across teams, even when their goals are aligned.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>Transforming&nbsp;patch management&nbsp;with autonomous endpoint management&nbsp;&nbsp;</h2>

<p>AI and automation address the core disconnects in&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/effective-modern-patch-management-processes-and-best-practices-for-patch-operations">patch management</a>&nbsp;by unifying visibility and reducing manual coordination. When endpoint discovery, vulnerability data, device&nbsp;health&nbsp;and&nbsp;patch status are correlated into a unified view, IT and security teams can work from the same facts instead of reconciling partial data across tools.&nbsp;</p>

<p><a href="https://www.ivanti.com/autonomous-endpoint-management">Autonomous endpoint management&nbsp;(AEM)</a> brings clarity to the confusion by using AI intelligence and automation to give IT and security a single, continuously updated view of endpoints, their&nbsp;health&nbsp;and their exposure.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<h2>How&nbsp;AI&nbsp;improves patching decisions&nbsp;</h2>

<p>AI improves patching decisions by prioritizing vulnerabilities based on real-world risk rather than severity scores alone. By factoring in exploit activity, asset criticality&nbsp;and&nbsp;exposure context, teams can align on what to patch first and focus effort where it will reduce risk fastest.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>With autonomous endpoint management, that same Monday morning scenario plays out differently:&nbsp;</p>

<p>The vulnerability is detected, and AI&nbsp;immediately&nbsp;cross-references it against a unified endpoint inventory. It&nbsp;identifies&nbsp;1,560 devices running the vulnerable version, including 217 devices that were previously unmanaged.&nbsp;</p>

<p><a href="https://www.ivanti.com/use-cases/automated-patch-management">Automated&nbsp;patch&nbsp;workflows</a>&nbsp;simultaneously: enroll the unmanaged devices, prioritize patching based on exposure risk and asset criticality. They then&nbsp;schedule deployment during low-usage&nbsp;windows, and&nbsp;begin ring-based rollout.&nbsp;</p>

<p>By the time the security team sends the alert, IT already has a real-time dashboard showing remediation in progress&nbsp;—&nbsp;with the same device count, the same exposure&nbsp;data&nbsp;and&nbsp;the same prioritization logic. No reconciliation&nbsp;necessary.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>How automation accelerates remediation&nbsp;&nbsp;</h2>

<p>Automation then turns those decisions into action. Patch workflows can be orchestrated end to end:&nbsp;identifying&nbsp;affected devices, deploying&nbsp;updates&nbsp;and&nbsp;validating&nbsp;remediation without constant manual intervention.&nbsp;</p>

<p>AI-powered intelligent patch scheduling minimizes user impact by aligning deployments with device usage patterns, maintenance&nbsp;windows&nbsp;and&nbsp;operational constraints. Ring-based rollouts allow patches to be&nbsp;validated&nbsp;on smaller groups before wider deployment, reducing disruption while accelerating remediation. The result is faster&nbsp;patching, less&nbsp;downtime&nbsp;and&nbsp;a more predictable process for both teams.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Self-healing workflows detect and resolve common issues automatically, such as restarting services, reinstalling&nbsp;agents&nbsp;or&nbsp;correcting misconfigurations. These workflows prevent avoidable incidents before they turn into support tickets.&nbsp;</p>

IT and security teams see the same devices, the same&nbsp;exposure&nbsp;and&nbsp;the same remediation status in real time.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>Shared patch ownership:&nbsp;powering IT and security collaboration&nbsp;&nbsp;</h2>

<p>AI and automation only improve patch management when&nbsp;they’re&nbsp;paired with shared ownership. When IT and security teams&nbsp;operate&nbsp;from the same endpoint data and remediation workflows, accountability shifts from defending individual reports to jointly reducing exposure.&nbsp;</p>

<p>A data-driven patch process starts with mutual goals. Instead of tracking success in isolated tools, organizations align IT and security around common metrics that reflect real-world risk and operational impact. This shared measurement creates clarity on priorities and removes ambiguity around ownership.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Effective collaboration depends on metrics both teams trust and act on together. Common KPIs include:&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Mean Time to Remediate (MTTR):&nbsp;How quickly critical vulnerabilities are resolved&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Patch compliance rates:&nbsp;Across both managed and previously unmanaged endpoints&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Exposure duration:&nbsp;How long high-risk vulnerabilities&nbsp;remain&nbsp;open&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Endpoint visibility:&nbsp;Percentage of devices fully discovered and managed&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>These metrics shift conversations from patch volume to measured risk outcomes and help teams focus on outcomes instead of activity.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Joint ownership requires workflows that span the entire&nbsp;patch&nbsp;lifecycle. AI-driven platforms support this by automating routine tasks while surfacing exceptions that require human judgment.&nbsp;</p>

<p>IT and security leaders define guardrails for automation, including&nbsp;approval&nbsp;thresholds, testing&nbsp;requirements&nbsp;and&nbsp;rollout constraints. Within those boundaries, automation executes remediation consistently and at scale, without constant manual coordination. Over time, trust in the process grows, coordination overhead decreases, and patching becomes a cooperative operational responsibility rather than a point of friction.&nbsp;</p>
