<p>Endpoint visibility has always been foundational to IT and security. You&nbsp;can’t&nbsp;secure,&nbsp;patch&nbsp;or&nbsp;support what you&nbsp;can’t&nbsp;see.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>But as environments have become more distributed and complex, what visibility means has evolved. It’s no longer enough to know that a device exists — IT teams and organizations as a whole need to understand its health, its&nbsp;risk&nbsp;posture&nbsp;and&nbsp;its impact on both security and user experience.&nbsp;</p>

<p>This is where&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/autonomous-endpoint-management">AI and endpoint automation</a>&nbsp;start to make a practical difference. By moving endpoint visibility from static inventory to continuous intelligence, organizations can shift from reactive discovery to proactive, even autonomous operations.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>Why traditional discovery practices fall short&nbsp;</h2>

<p>Traditional discovery practices were built for&nbsp;a very different&nbsp;IT reality. Their approach is designed for relatively static environments, clearly defined&nbsp;perimeters&nbsp;and&nbsp;manual processes. That strategy&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;scale well in today’s hybrid, cloud-first world.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Manual discovery workflows often produce incomplete or outdated inventories. Ivanti’s 2026&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/aem" target="_blank">Autonomous Endpoint Management Advantage Report</a>&nbsp;reinforces this reality:&nbsp;Only&nbsp;52% of organizations report using&nbsp;an&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/products/endpoint-manager">endpoint management solution</a>&nbsp;today, leaving many environments with limited centralized visibility and persistent blind spots across unmanaged or shadow IT.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>In practice, this fragmentation shows up in&nbsp;very familiar&nbsp;ways. Teams often juggle multiple inventories, one from an on-prem client management tool, another from an&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/products/ivanti-neurons-for-mdm">MDM platform</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;yet another from identity or access systems, leaving gaps that widen as environments grow more complex.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Common challenges&nbsp;in manual device discovery&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Manual discovery relies heavily on human input, which introduces inconsistency and error. As environments grow more distributed, these processes struggle to evolve with them, making it difficult to keep inventories&nbsp;accurate&nbsp;as devices are added,&nbsp;reassigned&nbsp;or&nbsp;accessed remotely. Reconciling changes across large estates becomes time-consuming and brittle, increasing the likelihood that devices fall out of view entirely.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Over time, these limitations compound.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/best-practices-for-it-asset-discovery-and-inventory-management">Discovery</a>&nbsp;becomes episodic rather than continuous, and visibility&nbsp;lags behind&nbsp;reality. By the time inventories are reconciled, the environment has already changed.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Visibility gaps and security risks&nbsp;</h3>

<p>These gaps&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;theoretical. Ivanti’s research shows that many organizations still struggle with foundational&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/endpoint-management-ownership-it-security-governance">endpoint visibility</a>&nbsp;even after deploying multiple management tools. Endpoint data exists across scanners, MDM&nbsp;platforms&nbsp;and&nbsp;access systems, but it is rarely centralized, continuously updated, or trusted across teams. As a result, shadow IT, unmanaged devices&nbsp;and&nbsp;unknown access paths&nbsp;remain&nbsp;persistent sources of security and compliance risk.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Blind spots create real risk. Many organizations struggle to&nbsp;identify&nbsp;which devices are vulnerable or even actively accessing their environments.</p>

<div class="flourish-embed flourish-chart"></div>

<p>When teams&nbsp;can’t&nbsp;reliably understand device exposure or access patterns, security decisions are made using incomplete or outdated data, increasing&nbsp;risk&nbsp;and delaying remediation. In fact, the above-mentioned Ivanti report highlights how common these blind spots are:&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>45% of organizations report challenges&nbsp;identifying&nbsp;shadow IT&nbsp;</li>
	<li>41% struggle to&nbsp;identify&nbsp;vulnerabilities across devices&nbsp;</li>
	<li>35% say data blind spots make it difficult to determine patch compliance.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<h3>Device discovery vs. device health monitoring&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Discovery is only the first step. Knowing that a device exists&nbsp;doesn't&nbsp;tell you whether&nbsp;it's&nbsp;secure,&nbsp;compliant&nbsp;or&nbsp;even functioning properly.&nbsp;That’s&nbsp;where device health monitoring becomes critical.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Discovery tells you&nbsp;what’s&nbsp;present. Health monitoring adds the context that&nbsp;actually matters, from performance and configuration&nbsp;drift&nbsp;to overall security posture. Research from&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/borderless-security" target="_blank">Ivanti’s 2025 Securing the Borderless Digital Landscape report</a>&nbsp;underscores how significant these visibility gaps&nbsp;remain:&nbsp;Two&nbsp;out of five (38%) of IT professionals say they lack sufficient data about devices accessing the network, and 45% report insufficient visibility into shadow IT.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>BYOD and&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/borderless-security" target="_blank">edge devices</a>, especially, are a concern. These can be online and still pose significant risk. It may be missing critical patches, running outdated software,&nbsp;drifting from&nbsp;configuration&nbsp;standards&nbsp;or&nbsp;suffering performance issues that impact users.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Presence data answers the question, “Is it there?” Health data answers, “Is it safe, compliant, and usable?” Without health insights, organizations are effectively managing endpoints in the dark.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Key indicators of endpoint health&nbsp;</h3>

<p>To manage endpoints proactively, organizations need continuous visibility into key health indicators.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>This includes:&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>Operating&nbsp;system and application versions&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Patch&nbsp;and antivirus status&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Configuration&nbsp;drift&nbsp;</li>
	<li>Overall&nbsp;security posture&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p>User experience signals such as crashes,&nbsp;latency&nbsp;and&nbsp;performance degradation also provide early warning signs that something&nbsp;isn’t&nbsp;right.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Modern platforms unify these signals into a single view, allowing IT and security teams to understand not just what devices exist, but how&nbsp;they're&nbsp;performing and where risk is&nbsp;emerging.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>The risk of tracking only device presence&nbsp;</h2>

<p>When organizations focus&nbsp;only&nbsp;on device presence, they expose themselves to both security and operational risks. Visibility without context leads to delayed detection, missed compliance&nbsp;requirements&nbsp;and&nbsp;reactive management.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Negative impacts on security and compliance&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Tracking presence alone increases the likelihood that malware,&nbsp;misconfigurations&nbsp;or&nbsp;policy violations go undetected. Devices that are not enrolled in management or out of compliance may still access sensitive resources, creating gaps in enforcement. When access decisions&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;tied to device state, enforcement becomes inconsistent by default.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Strong endpoint visibility,&nbsp;access&nbsp;and&nbsp;security ensure that only managed and compliant devices can reach sensitive systems and data.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Tying access to management and compliance status is critical. Conditional access,&nbsp;VPN&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/products/ivanti-neurons-zero-trust-access">zero trust</a>&nbsp;controls are only effective when visibility and enrollment are enforced consistently across endpoints.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Patch management&nbsp;is&nbsp;one of the areas where limited visibility creates the most operational strain.&nbsp;Our&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/aem" target="_blank">IT and security research</a>&nbsp;shows that many IT teams struggle to track patch status across their full endpoint estate and to stay compliant as environments become more distributed. For&nbsp;example,&nbsp;of those we surveyed,&nbsp;</p>

<ul>
	<li>38% of IT and security professionals say they have difficulty tracking patch status and rollouts.&nbsp;</li>
	<li>35% of teams struggle to stay compliant.</li>
</ul>

<div class="flourish-embed flourish-chart"></div>

<p>These challenges&nbsp;aren’t&nbsp;about patch availability alone. They stem from gaps in visibility into device state,&nbsp;ownership&nbsp;and&nbsp;real-world exposure, making it difficult to prioritize and verify remediation.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Operational inefficiencies&nbsp;</h3>

<p>From an operational perspective, limited visibility leads to inefficiency. IT teams spend time troubleshooting issues that automation could resolve, chasing devices that should have been discovered automatically, and reacting to incidents rather than preventing them.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Without health data, teams are forced into a firefighting mode, responding to problems after they&nbsp;impact&nbsp;users instead of addressing them proactively.&nbsp;</p>

<p>This is exactly where AI and automation can begin to change the equation.&nbsp;</p>

<h2>How AI and endpoint automation improve endpoint visibility&nbsp;</h2>

<p>AI and automation turn endpoint visibility from a one-time discovery exercise into a continuous, self-sustaining capability. They enable teams to unify data, detect&nbsp;anomalies&nbsp;and&nbsp;maintain&nbsp;accurate&nbsp;inventories without manual effort.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Unified telemetry across multiple sources&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Modern endpoint management platforms with AI and automation capabilities&nbsp;consolidate&nbsp;telemetry from discovery, UEM, MDM, patching,&nbsp;vulnerability&nbsp;and&nbsp;security tools into a unified, continuously updated view. This unified telemetry&nbsp;eliminates&nbsp;the need to reconcile siloed inventories and provides a shared, reliable view for both IT and security.&nbsp;</p>

<p>By normalizing data across desktop, mobile, server&nbsp;and&nbsp;IoT devices, organizations gain holistic visibility that&nbsp;supports&nbsp;faster, more confident decision-making.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Our&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/aem" target="_blank">autonomous&nbsp;endpoint management (AEM)&nbsp;research&nbsp;</a>also shows that organizations make the most progress when endpoint visibility is treated as a shared&nbsp;objective. Teams that track metrics such as time to discovery, percentage of fully managed endpoints&nbsp;and&nbsp;exposure duration through shared dashboards are better able to align IT and security around the same data. This shared visibility turns endpoint management from siloed reporting into a coordinated, data-driven process.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>AI-Powered&nbsp;automation and&nbsp;autonomous&nbsp;bots&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Automation plays a critical role in keeping visibility current.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/bot-library">AI-powered bots</a>&nbsp;can automatically rediscover devices, reconcile duplicates, update ownership and&nbsp;location&nbsp;and&nbsp;detect anomalies across the environment.&nbsp;</p>

<p>When agents stop reporting or profiles break, automated workflows can repair or reinstall them without human intervention. This ensures that visibility&nbsp;doesn’t&nbsp;degrade over time and reduces the operational burden on IT teams.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Self-healing&nbsp;workflows for IT&nbsp;productivity&nbsp;</h3>

<p>Self-healing workflows extend automation to the endpoint itself. Common issues such as failed updates, stopped services&nbsp;or&nbsp;configuration drift can be detected and resolved automatically, often before users notice a problem.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Endpoint automation enables these self-healing workflows to&nbsp;operate&nbsp;continuously in the background, resolving common issues without waiting for human intervention.&nbsp;</p>

<p>By resolving these issues without tickets, organizations reduce downtime, improve user&nbsp;experience&nbsp;and&nbsp;free IT staff to focus on higher-value initiatives.&nbsp;In fact,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/resources/research-reports/aem" target="_blank">over two-thirds of IT teams</a>&nbsp;today believe that&nbsp;AI and automation&nbsp;in ITSM&nbsp;will&nbsp;allow them to deliver better service experiences&nbsp;and&nbsp;give them more time to support business objectives.</p>

<div class="flourish-embed flourish-chart"></div>

<h2>Broader&nbsp;impact on&nbsp;security,&nbsp;productivity&nbsp;and&nbsp;user&nbsp;experience&nbsp;</h2>

<p>When AI and automation are integrated into endpoint visibility, the benefits extend beyond IT operations. Security posture&nbsp;improves&nbsp;and users experience fewer disruptions — and productivity increases.&nbsp;</p>

<p>By combining endpoint visibility and control, organizations can reduce risk while still supporting productivity and flexible operating models.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Closing&nbsp;visibility&nbsp;gaps&nbsp;</h3>

<p>AI-driven insights&nbsp;eliminate&nbsp;blind spots by continuously&nbsp;monitoring&nbsp;endpoint activity and health. Instead of relying on periodic scans or manual checks, organizations&nbsp;maintain&nbsp;real-time awareness of their endpoint environment.&nbsp;</p>

<p>This&nbsp;<a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/attack-surface-visibility-gaps">continuous visibility</a>&nbsp;transforms endpoint management from a static inventory project into a living, breathing capability that adapts as the environment changes.&nbsp;</p>

<h3>Improving&nbsp;IT&nbsp;operations and&nbsp;end-user&nbsp;satisfaction&nbsp;</h3>

<p><a href="https://www.ivanti.com/blog/how-ai-alleviates-help-desk-workloads">Automation reduces ticket volume</a>&nbsp;and accelerates resolution times, while predictive analytics help prevent downtime before it&nbsp;impacts&nbsp;users. Ring deployments, maintenance&nbsp;windows&nbsp;and&nbsp;self-service catalogs allow changes to be delivered with minimal disruption.&nbsp;</p>

<p>When users experience faster support and fewer interruptions, resistance to endpoint management drops and adoption improves. Over time, this creates a healthier feedback loop where visibility,&nbsp;automation&nbsp;and&nbsp;user experience reinforce each other instead of competing.&nbsp;</p>

<p>This is where&nbsp;autonomous endpoint management&nbsp;takes organizations next. Visibility becomes continuous instead of episodic. Automation keeps inventories&nbsp;accurate, health signals&nbsp;current&nbsp;and&nbsp;risk visible in real time.&nbsp;</p>

<p>With shared data and clear ownership, IT and security teams stop reacting to issues after the fact and start managing endpoints proactively. That shift from inventory to intelligence is what enables autonomous endpoint management, and&nbsp;it’s&nbsp;quickly becoming the standard for modern IT operations.&nbsp;</p>
